Introduction and Learning Objectives

Optical Illusion is an illusion caused by the visual system percept what appears to differ from reality. Due to the arrangement of images, effect of colors, light source, angle or other variables which mislead visual effects can be seen.

An object with four rounded shapes interconnected seen in the image of the left. If we placed in front of a mirror and the reflection of the object shape should remain the same. It is not, why is that? This is Optical Illusion. Due to the lenght, area, size, direction and seeing angle causes our visual system repsonds to environmental stimuli and how our brain then interprets this information. Which why we seeing the reflection shape is square not round. The learner will understand how optical illusion works.

Learning Objectives

  • Student will explore ways in which attention and preception related to illusion
  • Student will use their own reflections to build a peronsal idea about optical illusion
